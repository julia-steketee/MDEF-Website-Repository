What do I need to get started?


I figured the best way to start an experiment this week would be to first ask a question: What are my interests and what skills do I want to build? How can I start developing these skills. My immediate thought was bio materials. During the summer, I experimented with bio materials and used ingredients such as agar agar, saw dust, coffee grinds, damar resin, pine resin, and shellac. I did some fun experiments but I didn’t have the time to continue the experiments and never felt that I achieved a material that I could fashion a viable product from. Here in Barcelona, I am in a new space and don’t have all of the necessary tools to start a personal bio-material work station. My first step was to map out what I would need to get my research started. I made a list of some important tools and materials that I would be helpful to get started. I crossed off the ones that I already had but the next step was to buy, make, or find the other items on the list.

  • food residues (i.e. orange peels, coffee grinds, used tea leaves, used eggshells)
  • industrial residues (i.e. sawdust, construction waste)
  • glycerin
  • agar agar
  • sodium bicarbonate
  • corn starch
  • bio resins (i.e. damar, shellac, pine resin)
  • a dehydrator to dry organic residues
  • bowls for mixing ingredients
  • a kitchen stove
  • rubber spatula
  • mixing spoons
  • a kitchen scale
  • a toaster oven
  • a electric coffee or spice grinder

I went to Quimics Dalmau, a chemical supply store, to find the glycerin, sodium bicarbonate, agar agar, corn starch, alginate. Next, I started to collect food residues. Instead of throwing my tea leaves, coffee grinds, orange peels, and egg shells in the compost I started to collect them and dry them at home. I didn’t have a dehydrator so I figured I could dry them in at very low heat in my toaster oven. But I quickly realized that was not a viable option in the long term because it was requiring a lot of energy and I had to be very vigilant to ensure that my ingredients didin’t burn.

I will add more to this reflection...
